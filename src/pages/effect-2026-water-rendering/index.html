Premise

A surface becomes water through relationships.

A height field remembers motion. Normals turn that motion into light, and Fresnel reflectance makes the result respond to the viewer. Remove any one layer and the illusion becomes easier to read—and easier to learn.

Scope

This is a two-dimensional wave simulation, not a volumetric fluid solver. It stores surface height and velocity, but has no shoreline, buoyancy, or physically traced reflection.

01 / 03

Add energy. Keep the old waves.

A GPU texture stores height and velocity at every point. New touches add energy to that field instead of replacing an animation origin, so earlier ripples keep travelling and collide with the next ones.

simulation pass 
vec2 state = texture(field, uv).rg;
float laplacian = left + right + up + down
                  - 4.0 * state.x;

state.y += laplacian * 0.28; // velocity
state.y *= damping;
state.x += state.y + impulse;   // height

write(state); // becomes the next frame
02 / 03

Rebuild what the light sees.

Moving vertices without updating their normals produces a bent plane that still shades like a flat sheet. Sampling the height nearby reveals the local slope and gives every highlight a direction.

N = cross(tangent X, tangent Y)
Two nearby height samples rebuild the slope.

03 / 03

Let the viewing angle choose the reflection.

Water reflects more at grazing angles and reveals more of its body color when viewed head-on. A Fresnel term connects those two states without painting a fake reflection onto the mesh.

Shallow angle → stronger reflection

F = F₀ + (1 − F₀)(1 − V·N)⁵

Limits

Know what the shortcut cannot do.

Good for

Stylized pools, product backdrops, shallow interactive scenes, and learning how a surface shader is assembled.

Not enough for

Breaking waves, objects displacing volume, accurate caustics, underwater views, or reflections that must match a real environment.

Performance

Pixel ratio and mesh resolution are reduced on narrow screens. Rendering pauses when the surface is off-screen or the document is hidden.

Motion

The system motion preference is respected on first load, and the animation can always be paused without disabling shader inspection.
