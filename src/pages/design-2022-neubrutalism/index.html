Inspired by Neubrutalism

NEUBRUTALISM

Hard outlines and offset shadows make structure visible.
Hierarchy decides where strong color belongs.

In the early 2020s, neubrutalism adapted web brutalism's directness into a more graphic, component-friendly language: thick outlines, flat color, blocky type, and solid offset shadows.

Those explicit edges can make controls easy to locate. The risk is uniform emphasis: if every card, label, and action uses a saturated fill and full shadow, users cannot quickly identify the next action.

These three principles keep the recognizable visual grammar while assigning color and shadow according to information hierarchy.

Principle 01 · Structure

Visible Edges

The signature move. A solid offset shadow with zero blur. Combined with a thick black border, it separates a component from its surroundings without gradients or blur. A small translation on press connects the graphic depth cue to interaction. Move your cursor over the cards below and watch them lift.

Principle 02 · Color

Controlled Contrast

Saturated yellow, pink, blue, and orange create the style's graphic character, while a shared black outline makes different fills feel related. Assign colors to a limited set of semantic or emphasis roles; do not give every adjacent component a different hue.

Failure Case

Uniform Emphasis

Five buttons. Five colors. Five shadows. Which one is the primary action? Which one matters? Repeating the strongest treatment removes hierarchy, even when each individual control remains visually distinct.

No hierarchy
Four equal treatments make the next action ambiguous.
Clear hierarchy
Fill and shadow now distinguish primary, secondary, and tertiary actions.
Principle 03 · Hierarchy

Emphasis Levels

Keep the thick outline as a shared structural token. Then vary fill and shadow: color plus shadow for primary items, white plus shadow for secondary items, and outline alone for support.

The Rules

  • ✗ The strongest treatment on every element — equal emphasis obscures priority
  • ✗ Gradients, blurs, or soft shadows — that's another language entirely
  • ✗ Half-committed brutalism — if it's clean and polished, it's not this
  • ✗ Color as decoration — give each recurring color a role
  • ✓ The black border is the constant — that's what makes it neubrutalist
  • ✓ Use fill and shadow as explicit emphasis tokens
  • ✓ Three levels: primary (color + shadow), secondary (white + shadow), tertiary (border only)
  • ✓ Reserve the primary treatment for the most important item in a group
