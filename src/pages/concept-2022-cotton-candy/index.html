Concept Study

Cotton Candy

Meaningful moments earn a celebration.
Cotton Candy turns success feedback into joy.

Cotton Candy is a concept study inspired by the soft depth of claymorphism and the energy of an amusement park. It asks how an interface can celebrate progress without turning every action into a spectacle.

The triple shadow becomes a soft glow. The muted palette becomes cotton-candy pastels. The gentle hover becomes a bounce. Meaningful interactions can earn a moment of delight — a small burst for success, or fireworks for a milestone.

Celebration is feedback, not decoration. Routine navigation stays quiet. Completion gets the party.

Rule 01

Celebration as Interface

Effects are reserved for a successful result, not a generic click. A short burst reinforces that something finished; the text response still carries the meaning when motion is reduced. The cards below preview the feedback language.

Limits

Where the Party Belongs

  • ✓ Good for games, events, onboarding, achievements, and personal milestones
  • ✗ Avoid for banking, medical, legal, destructive, or high-frequency workflows
  • ✓ Confetti on success, fireworks on milestones
  • ✓ Keep routine navigation quiet and reserve bounce for interactive controls
  • ✗ Do not celebrate every click or animate static decoration
  • ✗ Do not leave particles running; cap and remove every effect
  • ✗ Never make motion the only confirmation of success
